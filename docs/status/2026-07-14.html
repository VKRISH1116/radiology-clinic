What I did today

  • Wrote the User Requirements Document (docs/URD.html): purpose, business objectives, scope, actors, and 20 traceable requirements (functional + non-functional) with MoSCoW priorities.
  • Ran a requirements-elicitation dialogue to resolve six open questions with the (proxy) clinic stakeholder.

  • Reconciled a conflict: brief wanted variable referral rates, stakeholder said "flat 20%". Chose a configurable rule engine seeded at 20% — keeps the feature and today's simplicity.
  • Baselined the URD to v0.2, then ran a requirements review: fixed a version bug and added 5 missed requirements (cancel/reschedule, password reset, slot availability + no double-booking) — bumped to v0.3; deferred "patient edits profile" to a backlog.

What I learned

  • Needs vs solutions: a URD describes what users need in the problem space, never how to build it — that keeps options open until design.
  • Traceability: giving every requirement an ID (UR-P-01) lets us prove later that design, code, and tests actually satisfy each need.
  • Elicitation surfaces contradictions: the "20% vs variable rate" clash showed how interviewing the stakeholder reveals gaps a written brief hides.
  • Stakeholder ≠ user: the referring doctor is central to the business but never logs in for the MVP.
  • Data-model signals appear early: the ultrasound price list already hints at Service → Appointment → Bill → Referral, the spine of Phase 3.

Decisions locked

  • Ultrasound-only clinic; 9 studies (Obstetrics has sub-studies); prices ₹1,000–₹2,000.
  • Patient auth: email + password.
  • Multiple studies per appointment; bill = sum of study prices.
  • Referral: configurable engine, default 20% of bill, "most specific rule wins".
  • Deferred from MVP: SMS/email notifications, online payments, DICOM viewing, multi-location.
